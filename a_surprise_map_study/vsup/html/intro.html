Background

For the purposes of this study, you are a marketing/sales manager at Company X.

Company X has a new product line that is being distributed nationally.

The primary sales metric is sales as a percentage of total county population. The product is performing well in some counties, but poorly in other counties.

Map Resource

Scroll Up

To aid you in identifying key counties, the Company X Data Science team has produced a map. This map includes a special metric, "surprise", which is a statistical measure of how surprising a sales rate is for a given county.

Sales can be surprisingly high, or surprisingly low. However, sales can also be high or low but not surprising.

Scroll Down

Unsurprising Rates

Scroll Up

Sales rates that are not surprising usually occur when a county has a smaller population.

For example, if 9 out of 10 people in a small county buy the product, the sales rate is 90%. This is not as surprising as if we were to find a county with a 90% sales rate, where 9,000 out of 10,000 people buy the product.

How Surprise Appears on the Map

Scroll Up

The Data Science team has produced a color scale that shows both sales rate and surprise.

  • At the upper left of the wedge are counties with low rates that are considered surprising.
  • At the upper right of the wedge are counties with high rates that are considered surprising.
  • At the bottom of the wedge are counties with either low or high rates, but are not considered surprising.
